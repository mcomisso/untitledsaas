# Landing Page Editorial Split Redesign Design

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**Goal:** Redesign the landing page into a more premium editorial layout while preserving the requested content and flow changes from the PDF feedback.

## Direction

Use a dark editorial look built around the existing charcoal and amber palette. The page should feel more like a premium growth advisory than a generic SaaS template. The visual language should rely on cleaner hierarchy, stronger typography, more negative space, and fewer repeated glass-card patterns.

## Key Decisions

### Hero

The first screen becomes a split composition:
- Left side: smaller logo, brand marker, short credibility note
- Right side: headline, supporting copy, primary CTA, permanently open waitlist form

This satisfies the requested “logo on the left, grow your youtube on the right” layout while keeping the waitlist visible and secondary.

### Mid-page structure

Keep the requested content changes:
- `Expert Coaching` is the second item in `What You Get`
- `AI Insights` stays last
- `Sound Familiar?` remains removed

Visually, reduce the repeated “same card” feel by making each section use a slightly different composition and density:
- `What You Get`: stronger 2x2 feature grid with editorial headings
- `What We Help You Master`: restrained text-led cards
- `Built for YouTube Creators`: premium product-brief treatment

### Close

Keep the booking form before testimonials. The booking section should become the visual climax of the page with a left-side explanation block and right-side form. Testimonials remain below the form, softer and less dominant.

## Constraints

- Keep the current dark amber palette
- Preserve the requested offer and copy changes
- Stay in static HTML/CSS/JS
- Avoid overdesigned motion or generic “AI SaaS” styling
